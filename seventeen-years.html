The Aspen Forum,
seventeen years of the conversation

2010–2026 · sixteen forums down, the seventeenth this August

Every August, government, industry and academic leaders gather above the tree line to argue out the year in technology policy. Seventeen forums in, the striking thing is how durable the agenda is. A handful of themes — the economics of innovation, competition, communications regulation, spectrum, privacy and media — recurs in nearly every year, and only AI and space have joined as genuinely new arrivals, both since 2023. What changes is less the themes than the questions inside them. The names on the panels turn over faster than the subjects do.

Seventeen forums

2010–2026 · more than 240 sessions across ten policy themes, including keynotes, firesides, panels and breakouts. The Monday dinner was a single keynote through 2017; from 2018 it became several themed Chatham House dinners.

Lines that run unbroken for seventeen years are the Forum's spine. The two that begin only in 2023 — space and AI — are the only genuinely new arrivals.
