The “Aspen Acres” fire is nowhere near Aspen

It is burning in the Wet Mountains near Pueblo, on the far side of the state, roughly 120 miles southeast of here and across the Continental Divide. It shares a name with the town and nothing else. It has not threatened Aspen, the St. Regis, or Aspen–Pitkin County Airport, and there is no route between the two that anyone would drive. You can see both on the map above.

Air quality in Aspen

Aspen sits in a valley, so on smoky days haze tends to settle overnight and lift through the morning. Smoke reaches here from fires hundreds of miles away, so how bad the air is has little to do with how close a fire is.

We don’t show a number here. The free ones aren’t measured in Aspen — they’re estimates covering a wide stretch of mountains, and they can be well off for the valley. These three measure and forecast it properly. Colorado forecasts Aspen by name, its smoke blog is written in plain English by state meteorologists, and the EPA map opens on the monitors here in town.
