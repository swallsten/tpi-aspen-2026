The TPI Aspen Forum · Between sessions

Adjourned.

Monday's on-the-record program breaks at two, and the working day resumes at six with a reception followed by Chatham House breakout discussion dinners. In between: four open hours, by design. The afternoon belongs to the mountains, and here's what forum regulars do with it.

"Where else can you watch a keynote address and then go fly fishing?"

Fly fishing the Roaring Fork

The Roaring Fork and nearby Fryingpan are Gold Medal trout waters, and August is prime season. The forum's signature pastime, waders optional, opinions mandatory.

photo Frank Kovalchek, CC BY 2.0

The Maroon Bells

Twenty minutes up Maroon Creek Road, and possibly the most-photographed mountains in North America. The hero image on our homepage, go see the original.

photo via Wikimedia Commons

The Silver Queen Gondola

A fifteen-minute ride from downtown to the 11,212-foot summit of Aspen Mountain. The one place in town with a better view than the conference room.

August in Aspen: warm days, crisp evenings, the odd afternoon shower. And remember, you're at 8,000 feet. The debate shouldn't be the only thing that takes your breath away. Drink water.
